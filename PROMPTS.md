# 📝 Testing Prompts for Lawyer.AI

Use these prompts with ChatGPT or Gemini to generate high-quality test data for the platform's features.

---

### 1. AI Document Analyzer
**Feature:** Extracts key clauses and risks from legal documents.
**Prompt:**
```text
Generate a realistic legal contract for a 'Software Development Agreement' under Indian law. Include clauses for 'Intellectual Property Rights', 'Liability Limitations', 'Termination for Convenience', and 'Dispute Resolution (Arbitration)'. The text should be roughly 500 words and use formal legal terminology.
```

### 2. Case Outcome Predictor
**Feature:** Calculates winning probability using AI.
**Prompt:**
```text
I am testing an AI Case Predictor. Provide me with test data for the following fields for a **Criminal Negligence** case:
- Jurisdiction: (e.g., Delhi High Court)
- Situation Description: (A detailed 3-paragraph story of a hit-and-run incident)
- Key Evidence Details: (Mention witnesses, CCTV footage, and forensic reports)
- Relevant Sections: (Specific IPC or BNS sections like Section 304A)
```

### 3. AI Case Builder
**Feature:** Structures raw facts into a formal legal brief.
**Prompt:**
```text
Generate a structured legal brief for a 'Property Dispute' case between 'Ramesh Kumar' (Petitioner) and 'The State of Maharashtra' (Respondent). Include:
1. Factual Background (Timeline of events)
2. Core Legal Issues (What is the dispute about?)
3. Points of Law (Relevant statutes)
4. Prayer (What the petitioner wants the court to do)
```

### 4. Legal Strategy Generator
**Feature:** Generates tactical advice for case scenarios.
**Prompt:**
```text
Create a legal scenario for a 'Workplace Harassment' complaint.
- Scenario: (Describe the incident)
- Main Concern: (e.g., Lack of physical evidence)
- Primary Goal: (e.g., Out-of-court settlement vs. Filing a FIR)
```

### 5. Moot Court Simulator
**Feature:** Immersive practice for arguments.
**Prompt:**
```text
Write a 'Moot Court Problem' for a law student competition. The case should involve a conflict between Freedom of Speech (Article 19) and National Security. Include 3 specific 'Issues for Consideration' that the student needs to argue before an AI judge.
```

### 6. Open Case Library
**Feature:** Uploading historical or community cases.
**Prompt:**
```text
Give me the factual summary of the 'Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala' case in this format:
- Title: 
- Year: 
- Court: 
- Main Sections involved: 
- Summary: (A 200-word summary of the judgment and the 'Basic Structure Doctrine')
```
